The Studio

A children's studio that grew into a second home.

Absolute Dance has been quietly building something rare in Midland, a studio where the goal is not the next trophy, but the dancer who walks in nervous and walks out steady. The mission, the studio will tell you plainly, is dedication, integrity, and commitment, in service of children who deserve good teaching in a place that feels safe.

What that looks like in practice is five rooms in a ten thousand square foot facility, a homework area for the after school crowd, and a team of instructors who know which dancer needs a quiet word and which needs a louder one. Parents notice the difference long before they can name it.

The studio runs from late afternoon into the evening, weekdays only, because that is when children can be present. It is open for ages two and up, with paths from first creative movement classes through serious technique and competitive teams.

The Faculty

Teachers who do this for the long quiet reasons.

Kristen Hollimon

Owner, Director, Instructor

Kristen started dancing at age three in San Diego, trained across every style she could get to, and carried that work to the collegiate level on the University of Tennessee dance team. The studio she runs in Midland today is shaped by all of it. Technique is taken seriously. So is the child standing in front of the mirror.

Alongside her, Bonnie Baeza directs the classroom side of the studio and Chelsey Epperson directs the artistic program. A team of twenty instructors and office staff keep the days running for families.

Most of all, this is a studio where teachers stay. That is the part dancers feel first and parents notice over time.

The Classes

Age first, style second, and a clear path either way.

Families come in two kinds. The first knows their child needs to move. The second knows their child loves ballet, hip hop, or something they cannot yet name. Both arrive at the same place. Start with age, then choose the style. The teacher will tell you the rest.

Ages 2+

First Steps

A gentle first taste of music, movement, and the rhythm of a classroom.

Ages 3-4

Little Dancers

Playful classes that build coordination, listening, and early dance joy.

Ages 5-9

Foundations

Structured classes with real technique, growing friendships, and first stage moments.

Ages 10+

Technique & Teams

For older dancers ready for serious training, expression, and performance.

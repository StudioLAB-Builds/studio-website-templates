Before you commit, the practical answers

Parent questions, answered straight.

The things families ask most often when comparing studios or starting a new season. Anything not covered here, the front desk is glad to help.

Email a question
Can my dancer try a class before we commit?
Yes. Families are welcome to visit the studio, watch a class from the observation area, and meet the teacher before enrolling. Reach out and the studio will line up a time that suits.
What age can my dancer start?
Classes are available for ages 2 and up, with age-appropriate options for tiny movers, elementary dancers, older students, and teens. Tiny Movers and Pre-Dance are designed for the youngest students.
How do we choose the right class?
Start with your dancer's age, then pick the style that feels most natural for them. Ballet, tap, jazz, hip-hop, lyrical, and tumble are all available. If you are unsure, the studio team is happy to recommend a starting class based on what your dancer enjoys.
What should my dancer wear to the first class?
For most classes, fitted dancewear (leotard or fitted top), tights or leggings, and hair pulled back. The studio shares specific shoe and uniform notes by class once you register, so you are not buying anything unnecessary.
How do term fees and payments work?
Tuition is billed monthly through the family portal. Recital and costume fees are itemized separately so there are no surprise charges. The full pricing breakdown for your dancer's class load appears in your portal at registration.
What do recital costumes and tickets cost?
Each class participating in recital has one costume fee per dancer, charged before the costume order is placed. Recital tickets are sold ahead of the show through the family portal. Exact amounts are shared in writing before any costume is ordered.
Do you offer sibling discounts?
Yes. Multi-dancer families receive a sibling discount on tuition. The discount applies automatically at registration through the family portal, so there is nothing to claim manually.
What happens if my dancer is sick or misses a class?
If your dancer is unwell, please keep them home. Reach out and the studio will arrange a makeup class in a comparable level when possible. Long absences for travel or illness can be discussed with the front desk on a case by case basis.
How do we withdraw or change classes mid-season?
Class changes happen through the family portal. To withdraw, email or call the studio so the spot can be reopened for waiting families. Refund and notice policies are listed in the registration agreement at sign-up.

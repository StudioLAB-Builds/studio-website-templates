A note from the director

Built around your family, not around the calendar.

I started Royalty in 2019 because I was tired of watching families get lost in big-box studios. I wanted a place where the front desk knows your kid's name, where the teacher tells you what they worked on that night, and where the costume bill doesn't show up the week before recital.

Seven years later, we're still a family-owned studio. We're bigger now, but the rules haven't changed. Class sizes capped at fourteen. Parents updated weekly. Costume costs locked by November. Pickup line that runs on time.

Yesenia M. Founder & Artistic Director

Questions parents ask

Answered before you ask them.

How does the trial class work and is it really free?
Yes. The first class in any style is free. Pick a class on our schedule that matches your dancer's age and the style they want to try. Arrive ten minutes early so we can introduce your dancer to the teacher. There's no enrollment pressure after.
What should my child wear to the first class?
For ballet and Tiny Royals, a leotard and tights with hair pulled back. For jazz, lyrical, contemporary, tap, hip hop, or acro, fitted athletic wear with hair tied back. Bare feet are fine for the first class in any style. Once enrolled, you'll get a style-specific uniform list.
What does tuition cost and what does it include?
One class per week starts at $78 per month. Tuition covers all weekly classes, costume fittings, family portal access, and faculty messaging. Recital costumes and tickets are billed separately and announced months in advance.
How much are recital costumes and when are they due?
Costumes typically run $75 to $95 per class and are billed in November so they arrive by April. Each costume includes accessories. Recital tickets are $25 per seat with reserved seating, on sale in March.
Do you offer sibling or multi-class discounts?
Yes. Ten percent off the second sibling, fifteen percent off the third. A dancer enrolled in three or more classes a week receives a five percent discount on their total tuition.
What happens if my child misses a class?
Tuition is not pro-rated for missed weeks, but we offer makeup classes in any equivalent age and style for the remainder of the term. Let the front desk know in advance and we'll place your dancer.
What age should my child start dancing?
Three is a great age to start with Tiny Royals, which is built for short attention spans and a lot of imaginative movement. Five and up are ready for structured technique classes. Older starters are welcome at any age and any level.
How do I withdraw if it isn't working out?
Give us thirty days written notice through the family portal or by email. The thirty days covers your next month's tuition, then auto-pay stops. No exit fees, no awkward conversations.

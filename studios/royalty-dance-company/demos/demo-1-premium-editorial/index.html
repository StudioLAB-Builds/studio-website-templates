A note from the director

Built to grow a confident dancer, not a competition trophy.

Royalty Dance Company opened in 2019 to give families in Fredericksburg a place where technique, artistry, and self-belief get equal attention. Our students leave class standing a little taller, whether they came in for one Saturday tap class or train six nights a week with our competitive team.

Every faculty member is a working professional dancer or choreographer. Every class plan is built around the age and level of the dancers in the room. And every family hears from us before they need to ask, because the experience your child has at the studio shapes the experience they have on stage.

Yesenia M. Founder & Artistic Director

Questions parents ask

Answered before you ask.

The questions we hear most often, with the answers we'd give if you called the front desk.

How does the free trial class work?
Pick a class on our schedule that matches your dancer's age and style interest. Show up ten minutes early so we can introduce your dancer to the teacher. If the fit isn't right, try a different style. There's no pressure to enroll.
What should my child wear to the first class?
For ballet and Tiny Royals, any leotard and tights work. For jazz, lyrical, tap, hip hop, and acro, fitted athletic wear and hair tied back. Bare feet are fine for the first class in any style. Once enrolled, you'll get a style-specific uniform list.
How long is a term and what does tuition cover?
Our season runs September through June. Monthly tuition covers all weekly classes, costume fittings, and access to the family portal. Recital costumes and tickets are billed separately and announced well in advance.
What is the recital costume cost and when is it due?
Costumes typically run $75 to $95 per class, billed in November so deliveries arrive by April. Each costume includes accessories. Recital tickets are $25 per seat and go on sale in March.
Do you offer sibling or multi-class discounts?
Yes. The second sibling receives a ten percent tuition discount, third sibling fifteen percent. A dancer enrolled in three or more classes receives a five percent discount on their total tuition.
What happens if my child misses a class?
Tuition is not pro-rated for missed weeks, but we offer makeup classes in any equivalent age and style for the remainder of the term. Let the front desk know in advance and we'll place your dancer.
How do I withdraw if it isn't working out?
Give us thirty days written notice through the family portal or by email. The thirty days covers your next month's tuition, then auto-pay stops. No penalties, no awkward conversations.
What age should my child start dancing?
Three is a great age to start with Tiny Royals, which is built for short attention spans and lots of imaginative play. Five and up are ready for structured technique classes. Older starters are welcome at any age and any level.

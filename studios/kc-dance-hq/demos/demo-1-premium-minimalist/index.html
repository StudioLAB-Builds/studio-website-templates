Everything a new family asks

Good to know before your first class.

How does a trial class work?

Book a trial in any style that suits your dancer's age, then simply come along. There is no pressure to enrol, and your child is welcome to try a few different classes before deciding what they love.

What should my child wear to the first class?

Comfortable, fitted activewear they can move in, with hair tied back. For ballet, a leotard is ideal but not essential for a trial. Bare feet or the right shoes for the style are fine, and we are happy to advise once you are enrolled.

How are terms and fees structured?

Recreational classes are billed per term in line with the NSW school terms. Adult classes are casual, with adult ballet at $18 and tap at $15 per class. We are a registered NSW Active Kids provider, so eligible families can use their voucher toward fees.

What about recital costumes and tickets?

The annual recital is optional but loved by most families. Costume and ticket costs are shared clearly and well ahead of time so there are no surprises, and they are kept as reasonable as we can make them.

Do you offer sibling discounts?

Yes. Families with more than one dancer, or dancers taking multiple classes, receive a discount. The full structure is included in your info pack so you can plan ahead.

What age can my child start?

Little ones can begin in our KCCubs preschool programme from around age two, and we have classes right through childhood, the teen years and into adulthood. If you are unsure which class suits, just ask and we will guide you.

What happens if my dancer is unwell or misses a class?

Please keep unwell dancers home to rest. We offer makeup options where we can, and our team will always help you stay on track if your child misses a week.
